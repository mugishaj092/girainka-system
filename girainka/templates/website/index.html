Girinka Program

A Rwandan initiative launched in 2006 to support vulnerable families by providing cows. The program enhances nutrition, increases household income through milk production, and promotes sustainable farming with manure. Families pass on the first female calf to others in need, fostering community growth and prosperity.

How it works

Cattle Distribution for Vulnerable Families

Selected families, based on poverty and farming capacity, are provided with a cow. This cow serves as a sustainable source of nutrition, offering milk for daily consumption, which is vital for improving health and reducing malnutrition, especially in rural communities.

Agricultural and Economic Benefits

In addition to milk production, the cow's manure is used to improve soil fertility, leading to higher crop yields. This not only enhances food security but also allows families to sell excess produce and milk, generating a steady source of income to support other basic needs such as education and healthcare.

Community Solidarity and Growth

A unique aspect of Girinka is its "pass-on" model, where each family is required to give the cow’s first female calf to another needy family. This promotes a spirit of solidarity, helping more families benefit from the program, creating a ripple effect that strengthens the entire community over time.
