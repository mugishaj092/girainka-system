Girinka

A Rwandan initiative launched in 2006 to support vulnerable families by providing cows. The program enhances nutrition, increases household income through milk production, and promotes sustainable farming with manure. Our Mission

To alleviate poverty and improve food security by empowering vulnerable families through sustainable livestock ownership. By providing each household with a cow, we enhance nutritional access, support agricultural productivity, and foster a cycle of community support, lifting families out of poverty for lasting change.

Our Vision

A world where no family faces malnutrition or poverty, and communities thrive through shared resources and solidarity. We envision empowered families who, through the gift of a cow, pass on opportunity, resilience, and prosperity across generations.

Our Story

Girinka began as a cornerstone of Rwanda’s Vision 2020, a national initiative aimed at lifting millions of families out of poverty and transforming communities across the country. In Rwanda, cows are more than livestock—they’re symbols of wealth, dignity, and community connection. Recognizing this deep cultural and economic significance, Girinka was created to empower vulnerable families by giving them the life-changing gift of a cow.

Through the gift of a cow, families gain a source of nutrition through milk, organic manure to boost crop yields, and a stepping stone toward financial independence. Beyond the individual household, Girinka strengthens community ties, as each family pledges to pass on the first female calf to another family in need. Since its inception, Girinka has distributed over 350,000 cows across Rwanda, impacting thousands of lives and fostering a chain of shared opportunity and hope.

Today, Girinka stands as a testament to the power of unity, resilience, and a vision for a brighter, hunger-free Rwanda. Through the commitment of families, communities, and supporters, we continue to build a future where every Rwandan can thrive.
